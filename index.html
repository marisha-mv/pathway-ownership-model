How It Works

The model has four layers. General Managers set commercial strategy for their business unit. Growth squads execute pathway-level campaigns and return data upward. Channel teams (Organic + Paid) execute at scale across all pathways. Infrastructure (Data + MarTech) serves the whole org and reports to CGO.

Operator, Not Relay
This is the role the entire model depends on. You sit between the General Managers (Rafay, Jaideep) and the 8 pathway leads. The Probation Stakes
This role is filled but new — and the probation period is the highest-stakes one in the org. The model is being designed around the assumption that this seat works. The 30-day and 60-day checkpoints are not formalities. They are the early warning system for whether the operating layer is functioning or whether the CGO is absorbing execution burden that should sit here.
What You Own
  • Translate strategy briefs from Rafay and Jaideep into 30-day execution plans with named owners and deadlines
  • Manage 8 pathway leads (4 Acquisition, 4 Retention) — weekly 1:1s, performance reviews, unblocking

  • Make the 80% of day-to-day decisions that don't need CGO involvement
  • Identify when a pathway is underperforming and drive the diagnosis and response
  • Work with Paid Manager on budget allocation — arbitrate pathway spend requests with ROAS data
  • Surface the right information upward: what the GMs and CGO need to know, not everything

  • Does the team move faster when they are in the room?
  • Can they receive a vague strategy direction and return with a clear execution plan within 48 hours?
  • Are they comfortable telling a pathway lead their funnel isn't working — with data and a proposed fix?
  • Do they create accountability without the CGO being present?
  • Do they think in outcomes, not activities?
Who Does Not Work Here
  • Strategists who can't execute
  • Project managers who track but don't lead
  • People pleasers who avoid hard conversations
  • Those who need the CGO in every important meeting

Structural Risks

These are the places where the model is most likely to break. Naming them is not pessimism — it's the prerequisite for managing them.

Risk 01
Head of Growth is new and on probation
High severity
The role is filled but the person is new and has not yet passed probation. This is the highest-stakes probation period in the org — the model is being built around the assumption that this seat works. If the person cannot demonstrate within their probation window that they translate strategy into execution clearly, hold pathway leads accountable, and reduce escalations to the CGO, the entire operating layer is at risk. The org cannot afford to wait until the end of probation to find out. Early signals matter: does the team move faster, are decisions being made without the CGO in the room, are pathway leads clear on their priorities?
Mitigation
Set explicit 30-day and 60-day checkpoints against clear criteria: execution velocity, quality of briefing to pathway leads, escalations to CGO, and team clarity on priorities. Don't wait for the end of probation to act on weak signals.
Risk 02
Rafay & Jaideep authority vs. reporting line tension
High severity
Rafay and Jaideep work daily with the CGO and brief the Growth squads — but report to Vishen. When there's a conflict between what the CGO needs and what Vishen wants, whose direction do the squads follow? And when performance falls short, who has the authority to act? Accountability without authority is a slow-burning problem.
Mitigation
Align explicitly with Vishen on decision rights before the org goes live. Document where the CGO has operational authority and where the CEO does.
Risk 03
Pathway lead seniority vs. role demands
High severity
Summit launches, funnel architecture, revenue ownership, and audience strategy are not junior tasks. If pathway leads are hired or promoted at the wrong level, they will execute the template but not adapt it when something breaks. The Head of Growth then becomes a full-time firefighter rather than an operator.
Mitigation
Keep roles open until the right seniority is found. An empty seat is better than a wrong hire. Interim coverage by Head of Growth.
Risk 04
Email orchestrator authority is advisory, not binding
Medium severity
If the Email Orchestrator can only suggest but not block sends, email fatigue will return. Eight pathway leads each with lifecycle flows, plus broadcast emails, plus summit launches, will overwhelm any audience without a single point of governance with actual authority to say no.
Mitigation
Formally document the Email Orchestrator's authority in the operating model. She can reject or reschedule sends. This is non-negotiable from day one.

Video Function

Video production sits outside the Marketing org under Gareth. All editors — social media editors and VSL editors — consolidate under Titus as the lead. Marketing briefs Titus; Titus and his team execute.

Reporting Line · Outside Marketing
Video — Under Gareth
  • Titus — Video Lead: Single point of contact for all video production needs from Marketing. Manages the full team of editors, sets production standards, allocates resource across social and VSL briefs
  • Social Media Editors: Short-form content — Reels, TikToks, YouTube Shorts, social clips. Briefed by the Organic social team and pathway Acquisition Leads on creative direction
  • VSL Editors: Long-form sales video production — masterclass recordings, sales letters, summit content, ad VSLs. Briefed by Acquisition Leads per pathway
  • All editors consolidated under Titus — no split between departments. One team, one lead, briefed by Marketing as needed
How Marketing Works With Video
Brief Titus, Not the Editors
  • Acquisition Leads brief Titus on VSL and ad video needs per pathway — format, hook, messaging direction, deadline
  • Organic social team briefs Titus on short-form social content needs — platform, style, reference examples
  • Titus allocates editors and manages the production queue — Marketing does not direct individual editors
  • Video briefs should come with sufficient lead time: VSLs minimum 2 weeks, social edits minimum 5 days
  • Creative direction stays with Marketing. Production execution stays with Titus's team
What Moves Out of Marketing
Editors no longer sit in Marketing
  • Social media editors — previously embedded in or adjacent to the social team — move fully under Titus
  • VSL editors — previously managed ad hoc across campaigns — move fully under Titus
  • This removes production management from Marketing's remit. Pathway leads and the Organic team focus on strategy and creative direction — not chasing edit timelines
What Stays in Marketing
Creative direction remains here
  • Script writing and creative briefs for VSLs — owned by Acquisition Leads with AI assistance
  • Social content strategy and platform direction — owned by the Organic social team
  • Approval of final edits before publish — stays with the relevant pathway lead or Organic Lead
